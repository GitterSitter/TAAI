Isr Med Assoc J. 2002 Mar;4(3):209-12.Mechanical alternatives to the human heart: intracorporeal assist systems andtotal artificial heart.Lavee J(1), Paz Y.Author information: (1)Heart Transplantation Unit, Department of Cardiac Surgery, Sheba MedicalCenter, Tel Hashomer, Israel. jaylavee@netvision.net.ilThe currently available intracorporeal assist systems and total artificial heart provide long-term support for patients terminally ill with congestive heartfailure, often in an out-of-hospital environment. While they are currently widelyused mainly as a long-term bridge to heart transplantation, their inherent risks of infection or stroke are prompting interest in future devices that will besmaller, fully implantable and hopefully stroke-free. These devices will bedescribed in the last part of this review.
