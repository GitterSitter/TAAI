Crit Care Nurs Q. 2007 Apr-Jun;30(2):104-18; quiz 119-20.Ventricular assist devices in the adult.Richards NM(1), Stahl MA.Author information: (1)Saint Luke's Hospital, Kansas City, MO 64111, USA. nrichards@saint-lukes.orgVentricular assist devices (VADs) play an increasingly important role in the careof cardiovascular patients. Developed initially for support of cardiothoracicsurgery patients experiencing difficulty in weaning from cardiopulmonary bypass, these devices have been used extensively as a bridge to cardiac transplantationfor patients who are failing on medical management. Research has demonstrated theeffectiveness of a VAD as destination therapy, providing a permanent means ofsupport for patients with advanced heart failure who are not eligible for hearttransplantation. Applications for VADs are expanding and advances in technologyoccurring to support these new applications. This article provides an overview ofcurrent and emerging VADs and nursing management of the VAD patient.
