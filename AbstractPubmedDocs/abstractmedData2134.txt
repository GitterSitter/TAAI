Br J Hosp Med. 1989 Jun;41(6):520-4.Temporary mechanical ventricular support: Part 2.Graham TR(1), Chalmers JA.Author information: (1)Department of Cardiothoracic Surgery, London Hospital.Temporary mechanical circulatory support is currently indicated in postcardiotomycardiogenic shock and if necessary as a bridge to transplantation where noventricular recovery is expected. Which mechanical support system to employ as a bridge to transplantation remains debatable. Implantable devices presently usedare prototypes of proposed permanent devices for the treatment of severe heartfailure in patients for whom transplantation is not suitable or available.
