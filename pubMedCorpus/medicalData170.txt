Right ventricular dilation and paradoxic movement of the      interventricular septum on echocardiography establishes the diagnosis. Right      heart catheterization is useful in establishing the specific cause and aids      clinicians in management. Principles of treatment focus on reversal of the      underlying cause, optimization of right ventricular preload and contractility,      and reduction of right ventricular afterload.
